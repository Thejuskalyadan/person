The things you did that I will never forget

The day you fed me

The world felt heavy that day — the kind of heavy that makes everything blurry and simple decisions impossible. You didn't ask what was wrong. You didn't wait for a sign. You simply placed food in front of me, quietly, as though feeding someone who can't feed themselves was the most natural thing in the world. It was a small act. It was the most generous thing anyone has ever done for me. In that stillness, I understood your heart completely.

"The quality of mercy is not strained — it droppeth as the gentle rain from heaven."

Shakespeare · The Merchant of Venice

When you pushed me forward

There were days I couldn't find the point of anything — motivation gone, direction unclear, the simplest tasks feeling like mountains I had no business climbing. You never agreed with the version of me that wanted to give up. You were steady and certain in a way I couldn't manage for myself. You refused to let me quit. I borrowed your belief in me for months before I could build any of my own. That belief carried me further than I can ever properly say.

"Our doubts are traitors, and make us lose the good we oft might win, by fearing to attempt."

Shakespeare · Measure for Measure

You never once doubted me

Through every failed plan, every slow day, every moment where I was far from the person I was trying to become — you looked at me like I was already that person. Like the gap between who I was and who I wanted to be was smaller than I thought, and not a reason to worry. That steadiness is not a small thing. It is an enormous thing. It changed the way I see myself, quietly and permanently.

"Love is not love which alters when it alteration finds, or bends with the remover to remove."

Shakespeare · Sonnet 116

A letter I have written a hundred times in my head

My dearest,

I keep thinking about the day you set food in front of me when everything felt impossible. You didn't ask. You didn't make it into something. You simply knew — and you acted on that knowing quietly, the way people do when they genuinely care without needing recognition for it. That meal told me more about who you are than a thousand conversations could have. You see people. And when you see them struggling, you do something. That is rarer than you know.

There were stretches of time when I genuinely couldn't find my footing. Direction unclear, motivation gone, the gap between where I was and where I wanted to be feeling insurmountable. You never agreed with the story I was telling myself about those days. You didn't argue — you just held a calm, absolute certainty that I was headed somewhere good. I borrowed that certainty from you for longer than I'd like to admit. On the days my own belief ran dry, yours was there, waiting.

"How far that little candle throws his beams — so shines a good deed in a naughty world."
— Shakespeare, The Merchant of Venice

You have been that candle more times than you know. In moments you probably don't remember, in small acts you didn't think to catalogue. I have been wanting to say this properly — not in a rush, not because something happened to trigger it — but deliberately, clearly, because you deserve to know.

I love you in the way that has no performance in it. Quietly. Consistently. With the kind of certainty that only grows the more I know you.
